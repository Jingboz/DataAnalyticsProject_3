Solar Radiation Factor Analysis

Sunlight (solar radiation) factor in bushfires:
  1. Temperature: Solar radiation provides heat, leading to an increase in temperature on the vegetation and other combustible elements, which will make them more easily to be ignited and burned.
  2. Humidity: Heat will cause low humidity which evaporates moisture from vegetation and makes them more flammable. When humidity decreases to less than 30 per cent the fire danger increases.
  3. Precipitation: Solar radiation is related to precipitation patterns. Stronger solar radiation may lead to reduced precipitation, increased drought, and consequently higher fire risks.

Assumptions made for the prediction model:
  1. Considering sunlight is the major natural cause of unplanned bushfires
  2. There is a proportional relationship between the indensity of sunlight and probabilies of bushfires outbreak
  3. Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI) in clear sky is adopted in this model, which means the quantity of radiation received per unit area on a surface perpendicular to the sun.
